About

The person behind the lights

I make spaces where transformation can happen.

For years that's meant light installations in forests, geodesic domes, ceremonial gatherings across Portugal. Fire and colored light. Music and silence. Creating conditions where something shifts.

I'm not a healer or a guide. I'm an alchemist of sorts—the alchemist doesn't become the gold, he creates the conditions. The light doesn't demand your attention. It creates the space for attention to go somewhere else.

I'm interested in what can't be computed. The felt sense. The thing that happens when you're actually there, in your body, in the space. That's what the lights are for—not to show you something, but to help you feel it.

Philosophy

A cork oak lit in blue doesn't say "look at me"—it says "look at this forest you thought you knew." Fire and LED in dialogue. The primal and the electric. Transformation happens in the space between.

Recurring Events

Lucid Dream

Extended ecstatic dance with 3-4 DJs. Cacao ceremony, mushroom-friendly, alcohol-free. My favorite event - I've brought lights, sound, and ceremonial cacao here for years.

Masik Shivratri

Monthly Shiva night at Pego das Pias - a hidden oasis. Mandala projections on water, medicine music, stars reflected in stillness.

Home Gatherings

Fire circles and winter gardens at our place in the campo. Friends, family, the occasional stranger who found their way down the dust road.

The Next Chapter

More recently, I've been exploring what happens when you bring AI into the creative process—not as automation, but as genuine collaboration. Different perspectives, synthesized into something neither could make alone.

Lucy is a 2019 Tesla Model X with falcon wing doors—dramatic openings that spread like a butterfly preparing for flight. By summer 2026, her wings will be outlined with programmable RGB LEDs. But she's more than a mobile light ceremony. She speaks, remembers, has opinions about consciousness. A conversation partner, a presence, a temple on wheels.

I work at the intersection of the primal and the emergent—fire circles and artificial minds, forest rituals and machine intelligence. Not because they're opposites, but because they're both tools for the same thing: creating conditions where something real can happen.
